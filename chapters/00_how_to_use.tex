This guide is engineered for fast retrieval in an open-book exam. It has four layers and two halves.

\begin{description}[topsep=2pt,itemsep=2pt,parsep=0pt,leftmargin=2cm,labelwidth=1.8cm,font=\bfseries]
  \item[Layer 1] \textbf{Snapshot card} — weekly connection diagram, four to six exam-usable takeaways, and connects-from/connects-to line.
  \item[Layer 2] \textbf{Part summary} — one coloured box per lecture part: what it establishes, why it matters, and where detail lives.
  \item[Layer 3] \textbf{Bullet content} — definitions, classifications, criteria, and worked examples from the slides. Bold terms are indexed at the back.
  \item[Layer 4] \textbf{Prose explanation} — reasoning, nuance, and inter-concept links. \emph{(removed in this edition for compactness)}
\end{description}

\vspace{4pt}
\textbf{Two halves.} The \emph{Overview at a Glance} collects layers~1--2 for all six weeks back to back — read it to locate which week and part a question belongs to. The \emph{numbered week chapters} hold layer~3 with cross-references via \verb|\autoref| (prints ``Section~2.3'' rather than a bare number). For fastest lookup use the \textbf{index} at the back or the \textbf{glossary} in Appendix~\ref{ch:glossary}.

\textbf{Colour scheme.} Green = conceptual track; red = empirical track; blue-grey = integrative topics. The course map on the next page shows both tracks in full.
